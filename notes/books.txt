Books on My Mind

Atomic Habits by James Clear — I keep coming back to the idea that systems beat
goals. I don't want to "get fit" I want to build a system where fitness is the
natural outcome. Same applies to content creation. Don't set a goal to post 50
videos. Build a system where one video per month is frictionless.

The Psychology of Money by Morgan Housel — the chapter on reasonable vs rational
is the one I think about most. Being perfectly rational with money is less important
than being consistently reasonable. A strategy you can stick to beats an optimal
strategy you abandon during a market crash.

Can't Hurt Me by David Goggins — haven't started it yet but I keep hearing it
referenced by people I respect. The 40% rule concept — that when your mind says
stop you are only at 40% of your actual capacity — maps directly to what I learned
in the Army. Worth reading before I make a video about resilience.
