Financial Independence Notes

The core idea from The Simple Path to Wealth is simple: spend less than you earn,
invest the difference in a total market index fund, and leave it alone. Time does
the work. JL Collins calls this the F-You Money concept — not about being rich,
but about having enough runway that you can walk away from anything that doesn't
serve you.

My FI number is 25x my annual expenses. At my current savings rate I'm tracking
to hit it in 11 years. Every raise I get I invest 80% of it and lifestyle inflate
the other 20%. The goal is financial independence by 50, not retirement — I want
the option, not the obligation to stop working.

Key principle: the market will drop. It always recovers. Selling during a downturn
is the only way to permanently lose money in an index fund. Stay the course.
